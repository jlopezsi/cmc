Nuestro propsito es investigar cmo el orden social influye en el consumo y la produccin de las expresiones culturales. Epistemologcamente partimos de la premisa de que el conocimiento est socialmente construido y la sociedad jerrquicamente organizada; por ello el consumo, una actividad simblica que nos relaciona con otros individuos y que hace visible las diferencias sociales, es de esperar que est jerarquizado de una manera homloga a como lo est la sociedad, facilitando as la reproduccin de las diferencias sociales. La teora sugiere que el consumo, y tambin la produccin, mostrar una organizacin social jerarquizada de manera homloga a como lo est la sociedad. Este propsito de investigacin requiere una visin interdisciplinaria y se concreta en estos momentos en los siguientes proyectos de investigacin: 1) La estratificacin de la cultura, 2) La experiencia de ocio, 3) Computater Assisted Qualitative Data Anaalysis (CAQDAS), 4) Computational Social Sciences.

The stratifiction of culture

pereat?

The stratification of leisure stream of research is based on P. Bourdieu (1979) groundbreaking monograph, La Distiction, published in english in 1984. Bourdieu (1979/1984) has developed a social theory of cultural practices grounded on the data he gathered about the French in the 70s. Making use of Geometrical Data Analysis methods, concretely MCA, he developed a cultural space of French lifestyles. Then he related the social to the lifestyles space through the conscept of habitus. So individuals in priviledged social positions exibit and habit of distinction and individuals in underpriviledged social positions, one of necessity.

The numerous contributions made by Bourdieu (1979) have placed consumption, and especially the consumption of art, in a social and political context of production and reproduction of social differences. Bourdieu and co-workers (Bourdieu, Darbel and Schnapper, 2003), Peterson (1992, 1997, 2005) and US colleagues (Peterson and Simkus, 1992; Peterson and Kern, 1996) have made the pioneering contributions necessary to place the cultural habits of consumers in Western countries on the social stratification map.

Our group has contributed much of the work of drawing the corresponding map for Spain (Lpez-Sintas and Garca-lvarez, 2002a; Lpez-Sintas and Garca-lvarez 2002b; Lopez-Sintas and Garca-lvarez, 2002c; Lpez-Sintas and Garca -lvarez, 2006; Garca-Alvarez, Kartz-Gerro and Lpez-Sintas, 2007; Lpez-Sintas, Garcia-Alvarez and Filimon 2008; Lpez-Sintas and Katz-Gerro 2005; Filimon, Lpez-Sintas and Padrs-Reig, 2011).

Another research group has focused its attention on cultural expressions rather than society and has studied the production of meanings associated with art and culture consumption experiences. Since the pioneering work of Douglas and Isherwood (1979), who argued for and documented the role of objects in the production of meanings, interpretive investigations have studied the way in which objects are used to produce and communicate social meanings (Appadurai the two theoretical approaches that stand out in interpretive research into art and culture are semiotics and cultural sociology.

Digital cultural expressions are also notably absent from interpretive research into the meanings produced during consumption. As an example, consider the articles published on digital games in 2003 in Information, Communication and Society, Issue 4, Volume 6. Of the seven original articles published, none interprets the meanings produced during the consumption experience; rather, all are interpretations of games from the production and the media side. Studies that have addressed the consumer topic have been conducted using, not an interpretive approach, but the positivist paradigm and quantitative methods.

The leisure experience

In recent years, many of the traditional cultural expressions (music, movies, books, comic books) have become digital, sharing technologies and consumption patterns typical of the new digital cultural expressions, namely, video games, thereby merging culture and technology. The digital transformation of traditional cultural expressions and their extension to games not only has implications for the business model of the cultural industries but also for digital leisure and social uses of the digital technologies.

While industry and governments have striven to maintain control over traditional business models (as with the draft Sinde law in Spain), we know little of the use that the consumer makes, during leisure time, of both digital cultural expressions and digital technologies. Moreover, digital games were not deemed worthy of study until recently (Bryce and Rutter 2006) and, in Spain, were only recognised as a form of cultural expression in 2009.

Despite the economic importance of digital cultural expressions, they have been studied but little and in a segmented way, as follows: (1) the use of the information and communication technologies (ICTs); (2) the use of technology in leisure activities, recently including digital games; and (3) the consumption of cultural expressions. However, these three knowledge domains intersect when we study the consumption of digital cultural expressions during leisure time and spaces.

The purpose of this project is to study and understand the interdependence between the consumption of digital cultural expressions in different leisure spaces and the digital technologies used. We depart from two hypotheses: (1) physical and digital spaces are interconnected through the individual activities performed in both; and (2) the places where consumption activities take place have certain symbolic properties that imbue the consumption experiences in that space.

Computer Assisted Qualitative Data Analysis Software

CAQDAS offer tools that assit researchs with the procedures of involved during the process of conducting a qualitative research project, such as transcription of texts, managing texts, coding and text intepretation (content analysis, narrative analysis, thematic analysis, grounded theory, case and cross-case analysis, etc.)

[]

We offer practical support, training and information in the use of a range of software programs designed to assist qualitative data analysis under the Framework of the Advanced Seminar in Qualitative Research (SAIC). SAIC is a platfrom for debate concerning the methodological and epistemological issues arising from the use of such software packages.

We have cofounded the Spanish Associacion of Qualitative Research, Espacual. The aim of Espacual is to promote the best practices in qualitative research among the communitiy of Spanish researchers. It organizaces biannually the international conference Iberacual.

Computational social sciences

We have are contributing to the developoment of an open data analysis framework. We contributed to the international development of the editor for ethnographers, EdEt, initially developed by Iwona Kaliszewska (Warsow University) and Sebastian Kaliszeski (now at Google).

Currently we aim at developing a qualitative data analysis framework for the open R computational environment for data a nalysis (R Core Team 2014). The r-project is an award winning academic project for computational data analysis. Concretely we are developing an addon for transforming Rstudio user interface for R into a framework for qualitative data analysis. The framework should be open, collaborative in its development, design for working research teams simulteaneouly, simple, transparent, modular, be able to work with every kind of texts, and be integrate n the R computational environment.
